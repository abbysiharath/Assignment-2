    • Project Idea

    Overview


    Being students, many of us share the common difficulties growing up. These common difficulties could be:


  • Lack of communication between teachers and students
  • Unable to access or lack of learning resources
  • Relationship with friends and other students
  • Bullying
  • Organization, such as planning due dates, scheduling etc.
  • Lack of communication between teachers and students
  • Mental health problems

    • The main goal we had in mind in developing this app is to improve the quality of life for students. The needs and wants of a student's matter. Fulfilling these needs and wants can drastically enhance student experience and learning. The app is an educational tool that incorporates a better canvas, social functions, student collaboration, wellbeing, and many more so students can live their best educational lives without worries.

    Motivation


    The motivation for our app has come from many unique needs and wants that all of us shared. Like the ability to easily schedule meetings or activities with your group, easily find great study areas to meet up in and even find wonderful places to eat plus many more quality-of-life things we wanted. The most important part for us was that this needed to be in one app, we wanted to make the ultimate education app for everyone to use.

    Description


  • Search for your school (primary school, high school, university, Tafe, college etc.)

    • To access the app, students should search for their school. This feature may be separated into level of education before allowing students to select their institution. Like many software we currently use as part of education, students (and staff) will be required to verify their education status to ensure the application is restricted to use by its intended audience, free from interference from those outside of it.


  • ^^^App registers you into your chosen subjects. E.g., Introduction to IT
  • Accessing live classes

    • The app should be compatible with services such as microsoft teams or collaborate ultra so that students can attend virtual classes – tutorials, lectorials, practicals and workshops. This feature of the app would be great for students on the run, maybe having clashes in their timetable or an unexpected work schedule; they can join the class from the comfort of their phone. A phone will limit participation in the actual class work, but we cannot dismiss attendance as a key factor of students’ education. This being said, there can be an additional feature which will record attendance of students for teachers, highlighting roughly how much of a single class they attended as well as their participation in the chat.


  • Calendar to schedule meetings, mark dates for deadlines or plan your days

    • The most difficult part for us when working together was finding the best time to have meetings. Everyone was always busy so there were very select timeslots we could have them in. And we needed to have constant and clear communication to organize these meetings. If we had access to everyone’s schedules, we could have easily found a time that suited everybody. We then discussed how it would also have been extremely helpful if we could connect these schedules to our university courses so we could all see when each other's classes were.


  • Making friends

    • We also wanted a way for students to easily find places to eat, talk with each other, and meet after classes. We wanted this to be easy to use and easy to understand. It also needed to be easily accessible otherwise people could not use it on the go, and it needed to be on something that people use every single day. So, we decided that an app on phones and computers would be the best and most convenient choice for everyone. We wanted to create something that helps all students whether it is as small as finding a place to eat or as big as organizing their work and schedules to be more efficient. It would be the one app that all students need to improve their schooling experience.


  • Clubs & societies

    • Part of the university experience involves immersing yourself in its culture; there are hundreds of clubs and societies to accommodate any student. Participating in such a community allows you to obtain the knowledge, skills and experience in areas such as leadership, communication and problem-solving. If you apply for membership in a specialized role, for example a secretary, you can build important skills in finance, public speaking, group development and management. Providing students with a feature dedicated to clubs and societies will encourage participation in the school environment, and for some even unlock a whole new aspect of education that they would usually look past and never consider.


  • More organized assessment system

    • We also wanted to do something that would clearly outline what work we needed to do and organize times that we could work on assignments, this would help to organize our workflow and improve our productivity. We also thought that we should create something that could be helpful to busy students to help organize themselves a bit better. We thought that if we found a way to display all the assessment tasks and the module work in a clearer and simpler system that would break them up into smaller tasks, it would make all the course work seem far less intimidating for everyone. The app should include a feature allowing students to search for classes and assignments, as well as a clear and simple way to upload files/notes to assist other peers. With this, students should also be able to submit assignments through the app by connecting to other cloud spaces such as google drive, OneDrive and any other services they may have used to complete their work.


  • Study spaces

    • We discussed how difficult it was to find a great study spot for group work that we needed to do in person with other classes. We would walk around for ages trying to find a study area that was not crowded, or we could not decide the best spot that had the least amount of distractions, So we wanted to make that process far easier for everyone, and we also did not want to be limited to study areas that are at RMIT, we wanted to be able to find spots all throughout Melbourne so that people could find a study area closer to where they live.


  • Locating teachers

    • In this app will be a feature to locate teachers and other staff. How often students go onto the ‘teaching team’ tab on canvas, yet they cannot find an email or even name for their lecturers or tutors. Making available a point of contact between teachers and students will most definitely improve the learning environment. The most significant aspect in student engagement and involvement is frequent student-teacher contact both within and outside of the class. Teachers can also be a point of support for students, whether it is related to work, conflicts with other peers, or for help with personal aspects of their life. Knowing a few teachers well increases a student’s dedication to their education, encouraging them to consider their personal values as well as future ambitions. Prompt contact with an instructor itself has been proven to be a key factor in fruitful and impactful learning. In saying this, the app should also include security measures, or even punishment systems, in place to protect teachers so that their contact details will not be abused.
