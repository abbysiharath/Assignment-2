    What differentiates each position from the others, if anything?

    We all have different ideal jobs that are associated with the IT industry. Each of the groups' ideal job positions have distinct roles, yet common elements with each other. As represented on the table, there are many common key requirements and skills needed to be accepted into the specific field. E.g. Everyone has a common element of Microsoft windows. The most common skill required in the IT industry is needing the skill to use Microsoft windows. Although there are many common elements between the group’s positions, there are many different elements as well. E.g., Skills such as marketing, network creations etc. are not common elements within our ideal jobs, as the jobs do not require those specifically needed skills.

    How similar or different are your career plans across the group?

    Our group's career paths are pretty similar. Although each person is vying for a distinct position, they share similar talents and qualities. We discovered as a group that core abilities such as the ability to utilize Microsoft and programming knowledge are interchangeable in the IT business.

    After looking at the skills and requirements across our ideal jobs, it can be found that there is a common preference for soft skills such as communication, time management and ability to work in a team environment. In regards to hard skills, we all need experience in Microsoft (windows, excel etc.) to begin with. As the jobs become more specific they require knowledge in java, html, completion of certificates (CCNA) and of course multiple years of experience before being able to acquire a full time position. The suitable tasks for the group span from data and system management to programming. As a front-end developer, you must be fluent in coding languages such as HTML, JavaScript, and CSS. This may be different for the work of a digital strategist, which demands a stronger understanding of marketing. While both are IT-related, they are very different occupations with very different tasks and responsibilities.

    As for career plans, it seems we’re all working towards completing our program at university first, to gain the knowledge and skills required for the IT industry as well as those specific to our ideal positions. Internships would help a lot as we’d gain experience in a workplace and provide an insight into how our lives would be in the industry. From studying at RMIT we are hoping to learn how to solve, support, troubleshoot, and build everything from websites to database networks. Participating in work-integrated learning activities will allow us to obtain hands-on industry experience and build a professional network. Some of us are not set on a future job and are keeping an open mind, since many skills we learn will be transferrable across most roles in the IT industry, but we are hoping by the end of the program we’ll be able to choose one that represents emerging technology trends and, of course, one that we enjoy.
